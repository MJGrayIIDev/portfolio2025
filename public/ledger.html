Ledger

Ledger is a legislative and political resource app for New York State Legislative Politicos. Users are able to get real time notifications, legislative news, and information.

My Role

End to End UX/UI Designer

Tools Used

Figma, Jira, Maze

Responsibilites

Competitive Analysis, User Research, Brand Design, Wireframing, Prototyping, Usability Testing.

Background

In 2017, two private companies held a monopoly on real-time information and notifications for the New York State Legislature. Both of these companies only provided one enterprise level subscription which started out at a minimum of $500 a month.

Challenge

Currently, there is no app in the market that provides native (device) resources at an individual level of subscription cost. Many need to create a de facto stack which consists of numerous apps and third party resources to find and access information.

Hypthotheis

Lobbyist, Union members, and legislative advocates need a resource mobile app that includes everything they’ll need to navigate the New York State Legislature.

Competitive Analysis

StateWatch

BACKGROUND

StateWatch was created in the early 2000’s and has become a staple in New York State politics, for a wide variety of non legislative staff audiences. This audience includes lobbyist, state agencies, labor unions, trade unions and others. Statewatch labels itself as the premier legislative intelligence service.

CORE FEATURES

Search - StateWatch’s search allows users automatic and manual searches by keywords, subject or authors along with other search criteria.

Tracking - Tracking with StateWatch allows users to organize their legislation in more than six ways. The tracking also allows for notification via email.

Reporting - StateWatch creates professional reports to support users and their workload. The offer report for bill status, meetings, calendars, research, analytics, and video scorecards.

ACCESS

StateWatch does not provide any pricing on their website. However there have been reports of subscriptions starting at more than $100 per month. StateWatch also provides it’s services a la carte.

Legislative Retrieval System (LRS)

BACKGROUND

Founded in 1984 by the Legislative Bill Drafting Commission, LRS has two experiences one free and one paid. By law New York State Laws are required to be free and accessible to the public. This is accomplished through the free version of LRS. However, there is a paid enterprise version of LRS. LRS provides powerful bill tracking tools to support anyone tracking the New York State Legislature. The LRS mobile website mimics the desktop website and has issues with the responsiveness.

CORE FEATURES

Search - LRS has a basic legislative bill search. A search can be queried by bill number, calendar number, resolution number, keyword search and several other search queries. LRS also has member and committee directory for each house.

Tracking - Tracking & Alerts - LRS allows users to create customizable searches and alerts. LRS send users chamber notifications related to the Senate and Assembly. The alerts and notifications are accessible via email.

Reporting - LRS allows users to generate automated reports on a weekly basis. The system features the ability for users to track specific legislation within folders users can create in their tracking system.

ACCESS

LRS features a free version with a basic web search. However the full access, including the features listed about cost $2,500 per Legislative Session or $209 per month/ per account.

New York State Senate

BACKGROUND

Updated in 2015 with a price tag of approximately $525,000, the New York State Senate website is the official website of the New York State Senate. The update to the website broke the digital connection between the Senate and the State Assembly websites and create two separate experiences.

CORE FEATURES

Search - The website offers the ability to search bills, resolutions, committee meeting agendas, session transcripts, session calendars, and public hearing transcripts.

Constitution Positions - Users can search for bills and can find the full text of the bill, the sponsor memo, committee history as well as voting records. If a user is signed in, they have the option to “vote” Aye or Nay on bill to express their support or opposition of the bill.

Educational Support - The website has a section that educates users on the legislative process specifically on how a bill becomes a law in New York State.

ACCESS

The New York State Senate website is free and open to the public.

Phase Five: Usability Testing

Testing

Usability Testing was an exciting experience since various stakeholders were excited to participate and see new innovations coming to the state legislature. The study provided great insights into improvement opportunities to the various features. Seven individuals participated and five prompts were created. The prompts reflected the established user flows and the features outlined. The results are below:

  • Users had an 86% competion rate
  • Four users highlighted improvements to the clandar feature(s)
  • All users provided feedback and recommendations beyond the prompts.
  • All users asked how the app would access and collect informartion.

Iterations

Before

Problem

There were several elements of feedback relating to the the detail view of the events. 3 out of 7 participants outlined various groups have events and they'd like to know who organized each event. 5 out of 7 participants commented or directly indicated they'd like to add events to their personal calendar.

Solution

I added a host section which featured the host name and contact details along with the number of attendees. To address the addition to the users personal calendar, I added a button icon located at the top right of the view port.

After

Before

Problem

6 out of 7 users indicated that a competitor included some form of annotation or note taking. The first version of the solution did not have the ability to annotate or take notes.

Solution

I included the ability to comment on various section of the legislation. Utilizing the anchor from the previous ideated solution above, I included anchor elements so the user can jump to the commented area.

After

Before

Problem

Certain bills can have more than 200 pages, which would result in the users constantly scrolling. The initial solution utilized was a text navigation and anchors to jump to various components of the legislation view. This would still require a high amount of scrolling on a small viewport.

Solution

I removed the anchors, and kept the fixed text navigation. Rather than jumping to various anchors on one screen, I created a segmented control that showcases the respective component.
